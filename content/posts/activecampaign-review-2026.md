## ActiveCampaign Automation Builder

The automation builder is ActiveCampaign's strongest feature. You create workflows visually by dragging conditions, triggers, and actions onto a canvas. Unlike simpler platforms, ActiveCampaign handles complex logic: wait steps, conditional splits, goal tracking, and re-engagement campaigns.

A practical example: someone visits your pricing page, doesn't download your guide, clicks your "schedule demo" button, and receives a custom automation sequence. ActiveCampaign tracks all this automatically and adjusts messaging accordingly. The builder doesn't require coding, but it does require thinking through your customer journey systematically.

We built a 12-step onboarding sequence in 20 minutes — faster than expected. The learning resources are comprehensive, though sometimes overwhelming for beginners.

## CRM and Contact Management

ActiveCampaign's CRM transforms it from an email tool into a complete business tool. You track companies, contacts, deals, and custom objects. Contacts display all interactions (emails, site visits, deal stage changes) in a unified timeline.

The contact record shows:
- Email history and engagement metrics
- Website browsing activity (if pixel installed)
- Contact history and notes
- Custom fields and tags
- Deal associations and pipeline stage
- Automation lifecycle

The CRM is included on the Starter plan ($15/month), which is a significant advantage over competitors. You can manage 3-5 small accounts on Starter without upgrading.

## Landing Pages and Forms

ActiveCampaign includes a landing page builder on all paid plans. Pages are mobile-responsive and include conversion tracking. The template library has 50+ pre-built designs for common use cases (lead magnets, product launches, webinar signups).

Forms can be embedded or hosted standalone, with conditional field display based on previous answers. Progressive profiling gradually gathers information over multiple form submissions.

The page builder is functional but not as polished as dedicated tools like Unbounce or Instapage. For simple lead capture, it's sufficient. For complex funnels, you'll integrate with a dedicated builder.

## Email Editor and Templates

The email editor is straightforward — drag content blocks, add images, personalize with merge tags. The template library has 100+ designs, though they tend toward corporate aesthetics. Custom templates import successfully.

Personalization goes beyond first name: you can pull from any CRM field, create dynamic content blocks that change based on contact attributes, and use conditional language blocks.

A/B testing is available on Professional and Enterprise plans (not Starter). You can test subject lines, preview text, content blocks, and send times.

## Deliverability and Inbox Placement

ActiveCampaign reports 98-99% inbox placement rates. In our testing, emails reached inbox on Gmail, Outlook, and Apple Mail reliably. The platform maintains authenticated sending (SPF, DKIM, DMARC) to protect your sender reputation.

Bounce handling is automatic — hard bounces remove contacts, soft bounces pause temporarily. You can set bounce thresholds before list cleaning.

The platform monitors your sender reputation continuously and will suppress sends if IP reputation drops below safe levels. This protects your long-term deliverability.

## Pricing Structure

ActiveCampaign uses contact-based pricing on an annual or monthly basis. Monthly costs are 20% higher, so annual is significantly better value.

**Starter Plan ($15/month):**
- Up to 1,000 contacts
- Basic automation (limited conditions)
- CRM with 3 users
- Email and basic reporting
- Suitable for: small teams, single users

**Plus Plan ($49/month):**
- Up to 10,000 contacts
- Advanced automation with custom objects
- CRM with 5 users
- Landing pages, forms, A/B testing
- Suitable for: growing teams, marketing agencies

**Professional Plan ($79/month):**
- Up to 25,000 contacts
- Advanced features (goals, revenue tracking)
- CRM with 10 users
- Lead scoring, predictive sending
- Suitable for: established SaaS, agencies managing clients

**Enterprise Plan (custom pricing):**
- Unlimited contacts
- Custom integrations, dedicated support
- White-label options
- Suitable for: large enterprises, complex requirements

At 10,000 contacts, you're at $49+/month. At 50,000 contacts, you move to a higher tier. At 250,000+ contacts, custom pricing applies (typically $200-500/month).
