Email marketing remains one of the highest ROI channels for business growth, but choosing the right platform can be overwhelming. We tested five leading email marketing tools across ease of use, automation capabilities, deliverability, and pricing to help you find the perfect fit for your team.

## The Short Answer

**Mailchimp** for beginners and small teams with a free tier. **ActiveCampaign** for advanced automation and CRM. **Klaviyo** for ecommerce brands. **ConvertKit** for creators and newsletters. **Brevo** for affordable power users.

## 1. Mailchimp — Best for Beginners

**Best for:** Solopreneurs, small teams, learning email marketing

Mailchimp is the most recognizable email marketing tool globally, and for good reason. The platform offers a completely free plan for up to 500 contacts with unlimited sends, making it ideal for testing email marketing. The interface is intuitive, templates are drag-and-drop friendly, and onboarding takes minutes. As you grow, Mailchimp scales affordably.

The free tier includes basic automation, segmentation, and A/B testing. The paid plans unlock more advanced features like dynamic content and higher sending limits. Customer support is solid, though the knowledge base is massive and can be overwhelming.

**Rating:** 8.2/10
**Pricing:** Free up to 500 contacts, then $13/month for 1,000 contacts
**Free trial:** Not needed — free plan available

**Pros:**
- Completely free tier (no credit card required)
- Excellent template library with 100+ pre-built designs
- Simple automation builder suitable for beginners
- Strong A/B testing and analytics
- Reliable deliverability rates (97-98%)

**Cons:**
- Free plan has limited automation capabilities
- Landing page builder is basic
- No dynamic pricing — pay per contact tier
- SMS marketing is limited
- Advanced users will outgrow free tier quickly

ActiveCampaign is the powerhouse for email automation. The platform includes a built-in CRM, making it a true marketing automation suite. The automation builder is visual and powerful — you can create sophisticated workflows without any coding. Conditional logic, lead scoring, and behavioral triggers are standard.

For teams handling complex nurture sequences, ActiveCampaign shines. Deliverability is exceptional (98-99%), and the platform integrates with 1,000+ apps natively. The customer success team is responsive and knowledgeable.

**Rating:** 9.1/10
**Pricing:** Starter $15/month, Plus $49/month, Professional $79/month
**Free trial:** 14 days

**Pros:**

- Built-in CRM with contact and company records
- Lead scoring and behavioral automation
- Exceptional deliverability rates
- Landing pages and forms built in
- 1,000+ native integrations

**Cons:**

## 3. Klaviyo — Best for Ecommerce

**Best for:** Shopify stores, DTC brands, product-focused campaigns

Klaviyo is purpose-built for ecommerce, and it shows. The platform integrates seamlessly with Shopify, WooCommerce, and BigCommerce. Pre-built automations for abandoned cart, welcome series, and post-purchase follow-ups get you converting immediately.

The analytics dashboard shows revenue attribution by campaign and flow. Dynamic content personalizes messages based on browsing and purchase history. SMS and push notifications are included, giving omnichannel coverage.

**Rating:** 8.9/10
**Pricing:** Free up to 500 contacts, then $20/month for 1,000 contacts
**Free trial:** Full features on free plan

**Pros:**

- Revenue tracking and ROI reporting
- Pre-built automation templates
- SMS and push notifications included
- Excellent Shopify integration
- Strong template designs

**Cons:**

## 4. ConvertKit — Best for Creators

**Best for:** Newsletters, bloggers, independent creators

ConvertKit is built for creators who prioritize their email list above all else. The platform removes distractions and focuses on subscribers, sequences, and revenue sharing. The email editor is clean and writing-focused, with minimal corporate design elements.

Key features include free tier with unlimited subscribers, subscriber tagging, and simple automation sequences. The Creator Network lets you cross-promote with other creators, and ConvertKit handles payouts for digital products.

**Rating:** 8.5/10

**Cons:**

## 5. Brevo — Best Value

**Best for:** Growing businesses, budget-conscious teams, omnichannel needs

Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) offers exceptional value — unlimited emails on all plans. The pricing is based on contacts, not emails sent, which can save thousands for high-volume senders. Features include email, SMS, marketing automation, and a CRM.

The automation builder is visual and comparable to ActiveCampaign at a fraction of the cost. Deliverability is strong (97-98%), and integrations cover most major platforms. Customer support is available 24/7.

**Rating:** 8.4/10
**Pricing:** Free with limited features, then $25/month for Standard
**Free trial:** Free plan available

**Pros:**

**Cons:**

## Bottom Line

Choose **Mailchimp** if you're just starting out — the free tier is genuinely generous and the platform grows with you. Pick **ActiveCampaign** if automation sophistication matters and you have the budget. **Klaviyo** is the clear winner for ecommerce brands. Creators should go with **ConvertKit** for subscriber focus and monetization. And if you send high volumes of emails, **Brevo** saves money immediately.

The best email marketing tool is the one you'll actually use consistently. Most platforms offer free trials or free tiers — test the interfaces with your team before committing.
